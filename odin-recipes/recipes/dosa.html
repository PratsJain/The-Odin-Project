Dosa

Description

Masala Dosa is readily available on street hawkers' carts anywhere in south India. But that is just Masala Dosa, not Mysore Masala Dosa. And they use the same Batter for all the Dosas. Also, for the Masala Dosa, they just put some Potato Curry in the centre and roast it well.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup soaked chickpeas
  • 2 tea bags
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp desi ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 green chillies
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • A pinch baking soda
  • 1/2 cup yoghurt
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. Take chickpeas in a pressure cooker. Add tea bags, 6-8 cups water and salt, cover and cook for 15-20 minutes or till fully done and soft. Drain the chickpeas, discard the tea bags and reserve the stock.
  2. Sieve together flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt and powdered sugar. Add yoghurt and mix well.
  3. Add some water and knead into a soft dough. Add one tablespoon oil and knead well. Set aside for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Add chole masala and dried mango powder and mix. Add reserved stock, mix and bring mixture to boil.
  5. Add dried pomegranate powder and garam masala powder and mix. Add Â¼ cup water. Chop tomatoes, add to pan, mix and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Deep-fry each disc in hot oil till light golden and puffed up. Drain on absorbent paper.
  7. Garnish chole with a coriander sprig and serve hot with bhature.
